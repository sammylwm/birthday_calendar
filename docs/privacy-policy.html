Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 17, 2026

Introduction

Birthday Calendar ("the Application") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information.

This Privacy Policy explains how information is handled when you use the Application.

Information We Collect

The Application uses Google Sign-In to authenticate users.

When you sign in with Google, the Application may access:

  • Your Google account email address
  • Your basic profile information (such as name and profile picture)
  • Your Google Calendar data, if you grant permission

The Application only accesses information required for its functionality.

Google Calendar Access

Birthday Calendar uses Google Calendar API to provide calendar-related features.

With your permission, the Application may:

  • Read calendar events to display birthdays
  • Create birthday events in your Google Calendar
  • Synchronize birthday information with your calendar

The Application does not access, modify, or store calendar data unrelated to birthday management.

Data Storage

The Application does not store your Google account credentials.

Calendar information is processed through Google's services and remains associated with your Google account.

The Application does not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties.

Third-Party Services

The Application uses the following third-party services:

Google Calendar API

Google Calendar API is used to provide calendar synchronization features.

Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information.

Authentication is handled securely through Google's OAuth 2.0 system.

User Control

You can revoke the Application's access to your Google account at any time through your Google Account settings:

https://myaccount.google.com/permissions

You can also uninstall the Application to stop using its features.

Children's Privacy

The Application is not intended for children under the age of 13.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.
